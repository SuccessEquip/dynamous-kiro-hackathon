Phase 1: Clarify

Define project purpose and scope

Think about the core pain point your project addresses. Be specific about your target users and their current struggles.
Describe concrete, measurable outcomes. What will be different in the world when your project succeeds?
Focus on your competitive advantage. What makes your approach special or different?
Walk through the complete user journey. How do users discover, try, and succeed with your solution?
Clear boundaries prevent scope creep. What related problems will you NOT solve?

Phase 2: Organize

Structure requirements and priorities

Focus on must-have features that directly support your value proposition. Avoid nice-to-haves.
Different users may need different features or experiences. Be specific about user segments.
Your MVP should solve the core problem for at least one user type. What can you cut while keeping the value?
Define specific, measurable metrics that indicate user success and project impact.
Establish clear criteria for prioritizing features and improvements based on impact and effort.

Phase 3: Refine

Analyze risks and constraints

Identify technical risks early. What don't you know how to build yet? What could go wrong?
Be honest about your actual resources. What are the hard limits you're working within?
Consider adoption barriers, competition, and market timing. What might make users say no?
Plan specific experiments to validate your riskiest assumptions with real users.
Define clear criteria that would indicate your current approach isn't working.
